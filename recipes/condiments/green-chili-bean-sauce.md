# Green Chili Bean Sauce

Batch-freezer condiment with roasted green chilis, beans, and aromatics - neutral base for versatile meal applications.

**Prep Time:** 10 min | **Cook Time:** 30 min | **Yield:** ~6 cups (12-14 portions)

**Freezer Life:** Up to 6 months

## Ingredients

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| High-heat cooking oil | 3 Tbsp (or omit for oil-free) |
| Large onions, chopped | 2 (~350g) |
| Garlic cloves, sliced | 8 |
| Ground cumin | 2 tsp |
| Ground coriander | 1 tsp |
| Smoked paprika (optional) | 1 tsp |
| Medium tomatoes, chopped | 3-4 (~400g) |
| Green chilis, cored and chopped | 490g (keep some seeds for heat) |
| Cooked pinto beans | 3 cups (~500g) |
| Vegetable broth or water | 1 cup (240ml) |
| Salt | 1 tsp |

## Instructions

### Step 1: Roast Chilis (Optional)
1. Chop chilis and spread on sheet pan
2. Broil until edges are lightly charred and softened

### Step 2: Saut√© Aromatics (8 minutes)
3. Heat oil in wide pan over medium
4. Add onions; cook 7 min until soft
5. Stir in garlic, cumin, coriander, and paprika; cook 1 min

### Step 3: Simmer (25 minutes)
6. Add chilis and tomatoes; simmer 10 min until thickened
7. Stir in beans, broth, and salt
8. Simmer 15 min, stirring occasionally

### Step 4: Blend and Store
9. Cool slightly
10. Blend smooth (or pulse for texture)
11. **Keep neutral** - no acid added at this stage
12. Portion into containers and freeze

## Serving Ideas

- Stir into lentils, stews, or grain bowls
- Mix with coconut milk or yogurt for creamy sauce
- Spread on wraps, sandwiches, or tacos
- Add to roasted veggies for heat and savoriness
- **Brighten with vinegar or lime juice at time of use**

## Notes

- **Neutral base philosophy**: Acid intentionally omitted for customization at serving time
- **Freezer portions**: Small containers or silicone trays for easy single servings
- **Heat level**: Keep some chili seeds for more heat, remove all for milder
- **Oil-free option**: Vegetables release enough moisture for cooking
