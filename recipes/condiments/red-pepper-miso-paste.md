# Pan-Cooked Red Pepper Miso Paste

Deeply caramelized, umami-rich condiment with slow-cooked peppers, onions, and miso.

**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 60 min (mostly hands-off) | **Yield:** ~2 cups

## Ingredients

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Red bell peppers | 4 large, seeded and sliced |
| Yellow onion | 1 large, sliced |
| Garlic cloves | 4, minced |
| Olive oil | 2 Tbsp |
| White miso paste | 3 Tbsp |
| Rice vinegar | 1 Tbsp |
| Honey or maple syrup | 1 Tbsp |
| Salt | ½ tsp |
| Black pepper | ¼ tsp |

## Instructions

### Step 1: Slow-Cook Vegetables (45-50 minutes)
1. Heat olive oil in large skillet over medium-low heat
2. Add peppers and onions with salt
3. Cook low and slow, stirring occasionally, 45-50 minutes
4. Vegetables should be deeply caramelized and jammy

### Step 2: Add Aromatics (5 minutes)
5. Add garlic and cook 2 minutes until fragrant
6. Remove from heat

### Step 3: Blend (5 minutes)
7. Let cool slightly
8. Add miso, vinegar, and honey
9. Blend until smooth (or leave chunky if preferred)

### Step 4: Adjust and Store
10. Taste and adjust seasoning
11. Transfer to jars
12. Refrigerate up to 2 weeks, freeze up to 3 months

## Uses

- Spread on sandwiches or wraps
- Mix into grain bowls
- Toss with roasted vegetables
- Stir into pasta for quick sauce
- Use as pizza sauce base
- Add to soups for depth

## Notes

- **Low and slow**: Don't rush caramelization - this builds all the flavor
- **Miso addition**: Add off heat to preserve probiotics
- **Consistency**: Blend smooth for spread, leave chunky for relish
- **Make-ahead**: Flavor improves after 24 hours
