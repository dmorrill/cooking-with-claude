### Production Notes & Tips

#### Equipment Needed (Large Batches)
- Heavy-bottomed pan or Dutch oven (6+ quart capacity for 3x batch)
- Two pans recommended for 6x batch
- Food processor (12+ cup capacity)
- Portioning containers or jars
- Labels

#### Common Issues & Solutions
- **Catching/burning**: Add water splashes more frequently in larger batches
- **Uneven caramelization**: Stir more often, ensure even heat distribution
- **Too thick**: Add warm water or vegetable broth during processing
- **Too thin**: Simmer longer uncovered to reduce moisture

#### Prep Efficiency Tips
- Use food processor to chop vegetables quickly
- Prep multiple batches of vegetables, cook in sequence
- Cool completely before jarring to prevent condensation
- Label immediately with date and batch number

### Quality Control Checklist

- [ ] Color: Deep red, jammy, glossy appearance
- [ ] Texture: Thick, spoonable, not watery
- [ ] Flavor balance: Umami-forward, bright acidity, no bitterness
- [ ] Consistency: Smooth with minimal chunks (unless rustic texture desired)
- [ ] Temperature: Cooled to room temp before jarring
- [ ] Labeling: All required info present and legible

## Notes on Building a Staples System

### Benefits
- **Menu flexibility**: Swap components between dishes easily
- **Consistent quality**: Batch production = standardized flavor
- **Labor efficiency**: Prep once, use multiple times
- **Revenue diversification**: Retail sales + menu use
- **Customer loyalty**: "I need more of that red pepper miso!"

### Considerations
- **Storage space**: Need adequate refrigeration/freezer capacity
- **Shelf life management**: Track dates carefully, FIFO rotation
- **Production scheduling**: Balance fresh-made with efficiency
- **Costing**: Track ingredient costs for accurate menu pricing
- **Licensing**: Check local regulations for packaged food sales

### Menu Design Strategy
- Feature staples prominently: "Made with our house red pepper miso"
- Offer "build your own" options using staple components
- Seasonal variations: Adapt staples to available produce
- Limited-time specials: Test new staples before adding permanently
